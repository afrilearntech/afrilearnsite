Projects

Check Some of Our Projects

The Ghana Learning Passport

Our flagship project, the Ghana Learning Passport, is a collaboration with the Ministry of Education in Ghana and UNICEF to provide access to quality learning resources for children and youth affected by school closures due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

  • Projected by UNICEF as a model for digital learning in Africa.
  • Designed to be scalable and adaptable to different contexts.
  • Incorporates a wide range of digital content, including videos, quizzes, and interactive activities.

Channel MoE TV

Channel MoE TV is an innovative educational platform developed in partnership with the Ministry of Education in Ghana. It aims to provide high-quality, accessible learning content to students across the country.

This initiative was spearheaded by our CEO, leveraging his expertise in digital education and technology solutions. Channel MoE TV broadcasts educational programs aligned with the national curriculum, covering subjects such as mathematics, science, language arts, and social studies.

Minecraft Education for SHS Students

This innovative project leverages the popular game Minecraft to create engaging and interactive learning experiences for Senior High School students in Ghana. And it's implementation is overseen by some of our expert educators.

  • Promotes creativity and problem-solving skills through game-based learning.
  • Aligns with the national curriculum to enhance traditional teaching methods.
  • Encourages collaboration and teamwork among students.

Technology-Enabled Open School System

The Technology-Enabled Open School System is a groundbreaking initiative aimed at providing flexible, accessible, and high-quality education to learners in Ghana. By leveraging digital technologies, this system seeks to bridge the gap between traditional classroom learning and the needs of modern students.

The project assessed the extent to which learners are equipped with digital skills and can access e-learning resources independently and evaluate the impact of ICT on teaching methodologies and learning outcomes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to some of the most common questions about our services, partnerships, and areas of operation.

What services does AfriLearn Tech Company Limited provide?

AfriLearn Tech Company Limited offers a full spectrum of digital transformation and consulting services, including Education Technology (EdTech) solutions, capacity building, curriculum development, media and broadcasting infrastructure setup, and institutional consultancy. We design and implement systems that help governments, schools, and organizations digitize learning, strengthen capacity, and enhance operational efficiency.

Where does AfriLearn Tech Company Limited operate?

We are an African company with operations and partnerships across West Africa and beyond — including projects in Ghana, Liberia, Rwanda, and Nigeria. Our solutions are built to scale across the continent, supporting both national education systems and institutional clients seeking to embrace technology for growth and impact.

Does AfriLearn Tech work with governments and development partners?

Yes. AfriLearn Tech regularly collaborates with Ministries of Education, public agencies, and international development partners on large-scale education, ICT, and capacity-building initiatives. We combine technical expertise, policy understanding, and local context to deliver sustainable solutions that align with national and institutional goals.
